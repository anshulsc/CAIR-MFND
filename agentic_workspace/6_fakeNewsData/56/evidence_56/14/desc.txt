This festival is as important to Hindus as the Christmas holiday is to Christians. Over the centuries, <strong>Diwali</strong> has become a national festival that&#x27;s also enjoyed by non-Hindu communities. For instance, in Jainism, <strong>Diwali</strong> marks the nirvana, or spiritual awakening, of Lord Mahavira on October 15, 527 B.C.; in Sikhism, it honors the day that Guru Hargobind Ji, the Sixth Sikh Guru, was freed from imprisonment.
